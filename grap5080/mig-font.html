Project 1: Migration Font Design

This project invoved creating our own typefaces from scratch. We were given the theme of migration as a starting point and asked to document our journey in a type journal. We had to design a typeface with the characters from A to Z in either upper or lowercase plus a set of numerals. As an extension to this project we then had to create a poster to show off our typefaces.


Initial Research

In the brief, we were given the theme of migration as a starting point. The first thing I did in this project was brainstorm the different meanings of migration. I knew I didn't want to follow the most obvious route so being able to get all my ideas on paper first was very beneficial.

Once I had these ideas on paper, I was able to decide which route I wanted to follow. This ended up being the environmental route. I wanted to explore more specifically how the tide changes the shoreline and surrounding environment. I spent some time at Allonby and Maryport studying this in more detail. Below are images of my journey so far.



Typography Research

Once I had decided which direction I was taking this project, the next thing to do was look at typography that some way incorporated my idea of movement and change. I looked in books and on the internet at different typefaces that conveyed this sense of migration.

Initial Inspiration

After I had looked at a few different typefaces and styles of lettering, I went on to gather further inspiration. I wanted to look more closely at the organic shapes that are part of the environment. I looked closely at seaweed and pebbles. Below are photographs, sketches and digital explorations of these forms.

Initial Sketches

Once I knew the kind of style I wanted the typeface to be, I went back to the beginning and began sketching and exploring few different typefaces. This consisted of cutting and sticking and breaking the letters down into single pixels.
