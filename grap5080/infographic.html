Theme – Shakespears Sister

For a long time music has heavily influenced my life. Once I knew that in this project we were able to pick our own topics, I knew I wanted to look at music.

One band that I have always found very interesting is Shakespears Sister. They are an alternative pop and rock musical duo that was formed in 1988, featuring Siobhan Fahey and Marcella Detroit. I think the thing that really stood out to me about this duo is that they used their music as a way to cultivate their own differences. They were never products of the time, they seem to stand slightly outside what was going on. They each come from very opposing musical and cultural backgrounds, with very contrasting vocals which became a defining part of their sound.

Another thing that I noticed about the band was the visual aesthetic. Many of their music videos were directed by Sophie Muller. The pair were heavily influenced by the films 'Whatever Happened to Baby Jane' and 'Sunset Boulevard' and that classic old Hollywood vibe of the deranged diva and the embattled woman. 'Hormonally Yours' was the duo's second album together. It was created as a concept album that was an almost soundtrack to the 50s B-film 'Cat-Women of the Moon'. The content of many of the music videos was derived from the films plot. For instance, the single 'Stay', which stayed at no. 1 in the UK Charts for 8 weeks, was influenced by the film. The video featured Detroit and Fahey as catwomen who were fighting over this earthling character.

As well as being known for their music, Shakespears Sister is almost as well known for their visuals. Sophie Muller exploited the difficulties between Detroit and Fahey in order to create groundbreaking pieces of art. She plays on the pairs mutual paranoia and insecurities and manages to create very real, honest videos.

The duo are infamously known for their 26 year feud. After years of working together, the pair split up in 1996, due to misunderstandings and miscommunications. Detroit and Fahey didn't speak for over 26 years until they were ready to repair their friendship and finally understand and forgive each other. Eventually they ended up realising how similar they were and how they still had that creative spark together. In 2018, the pair got together and were able to create new music with a new appreciation and respect for each other. The music video for one of their latest singles 'All the Queen's Horses', was directed by Sophie Muller. Muller was able to rekindle the iconic aesthetic of Shakespears Sister whilst also mocking their struggled relationship. The video is cleverly constructed, with flashbacks to previous videos. This helped to create a link back to times before the split and acts as a reminder of what has not been lost. Muller shows a sense of healing whilst conveying the drama that occured between the pair.

Notes



Siobhan Fahey

Siobhan Fahey was born in Dublin in 1958 and is a singer and musician. She was a former member of the girl group, Bananarama, but left in 1988 to pursue her own musical style and created Shakespears Sister. She joined the original members of Bananarama in 2017 for their UK tour.

Siobhan's inspiration came from many places. After spending so long working in a girl group, it took Fahey longer to identify her own style. She has been influenced by varied and diverse artists from Echo and the Bunnymen to The Smiths, to Bowie and Giorgio Moroder. Fahey began Shakespears Sister in 1988 as a solo act, but it was later suggested her and Detroits voices would pair well together. This meeting of light and shade was very new of its time and still feels fresh today.



Marcella Detroit (Levy)

Marcella Detroit was born in Detroit in 1952 and is an American singer, songwriter and guitarist. She co-wrote 'Lay Down Sally' with Eric Clapton in 1977 and joined Shakespears Sister in 1989. After leaving the band in 1993, Detroit produced her own music and had a UK top 20 hit with 'I Believe' in 1994.

Marcella was raised in suburban Detroit, growing up around Folk Blues and Motown. From an early age she was interested in instruments, learning the violin, guitar, harmonica and keyboard very young. She has always had a very purist approach to music. When Detroit moved to LA, she began to do a lot of R&B and Soul session work for people like Aretha Franklin and George Duke.



Initial Ideas

My initial idea for this project surrounded the feud and rift between the pair. I thought it would be interesting to try and visually represent the duo's journey, following the good and difficult times. I believe that the band are misunderstood, however their music still stands on its own today.

I thought about trying to represnt this journey through an animated infographic. I think that the animated form will convey this story effectively. I also like the idea of making it as a timeline as it will be following the band from the very beginning.

Intentions - Further Research

After much research into the band and their history, I decided that I wanted to aim the infographic at an audience with little prior knowledge of them. This would mean that I would focus more on the history, journey and relationship of the members and the band itself.

I became aware that I was possibly too invested in the topic, and was looking at what I found interesting, not what others would want to learn. I took a step back at this point and asked for other people's opinions. I asked about six different people if they had heard of the band and what they would like to find out about them. After these discussions, I found most people had some memory of the band, but in no great detail. The feedback I got was that most people were less interested in the statistics of the songs and were more interested in the journey and relationship.

I found this extra research very helpful as it helped me to understand, on average, what people wanted to know - not just what I wanted to show.

Initial Ideas

Once I had collected a few images, I began to plan out the order of the infographic. As I wanted mine to be interactive, I needed to plan it slightly differently to show each eventuality. I knew this would be a long process and many of my initial ideas would change so I used post it notes to illustrate the flow of the infographic. This allowed me to quickly add and move things around when I needed to.

When I was reasonably happy with the rough order of elements, I went on to mocking up some initial user flows. This helps me to visualise what the end product will look like and how the elements will interact with one another.



Style Tests/Exploration

In order to create a consistent piece of design, it was important that I tested some initial ideas. This would save me time trying out styles and colours at a later date. I decided on the most important elements of the design and explored the limitations of each. I wanted to look at image treatment, type choice and creating an interactive code.


Image Treatment

After I had decided on a rough order for the information, I went on to exploring how to achieve a similar style to that of the band. Many of the music videos were inspired by old Hollywood films like Whatever Happened to Baby Jane and Sunset Boulevard. The videos contain both colour and black and white imagery so I decided to play around with some different ideas until I was happy with the outcome.

I looked at black and white imagery as well as colour. I also applied different filters and textures to the images in order to try and achieve a similar effect. I played with the idea of having a slightly different style at the beginning and the end to represent the change in time and the evolved relationship. Below are my ideas for image treatment.
