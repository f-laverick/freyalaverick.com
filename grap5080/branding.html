Branding and Publicity

For this project we would be working in groups of 4. Each group was given a city and we had to come up with branding to promote the city. We were acting as a design consultancy, bidding for our city to be the City of Culture 2025.



Research

Once we had been given our groups and cities we began the research stage. My group were given the city of Bath. Before beginning this project I knew very little about the city apart from the fact it has beautiful architecture and is known for its literary figures.


We split the research into different sections to make it easier. These were location, history, industry, culture and architecture. We had to collate our research and create a pdf to present at a later date. Below is the link to our presentation.



Initial Visual Research

Once we had completed our presentation, I went on to look at other branding designs, especially those aimed at cities and places. This helped me get an idea how other people have tackled a similar brief. I like a lot of the more simple, minimal designs, with subtle use of colour.



Early Ideas to Explore

During the research into our city, we found that Bath is possibly most well known for the Roman Baths and its iconic architecture. Architecture has been a passion of mine for many years, and as it has such strong connections to Bath, it is a route we would be likely to explore further. We also found out that Bath is a hub of literary figure, some of which being Jane Austen, Mary Shelley and Charles Dickens. As Bath has the Jane Austen Centre and attracts many tourists for this reason, it is another avenue we thought about exploring. Below are moodboards exloring architecture and literature in Bath.


Architecture

With regards to architecture, I took the topic from two different directions. I began looking at the different architectural features in more detail, this involved the Classical orders as seen on the Circus, to the fan vaulting seen on the Abbey. I then started looking at some of the popular architectural landmarks within the city. These ranged from Pulteney Bridge to the Royal Crescent.


Initial Ideas
