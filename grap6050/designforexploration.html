Design for Exploration

To begin our third year, we were given a two week task. We were each given a word and had to complete a series of experimental tasks based on the word.



Day 1

I was given the word 'flash'. I began by looking at the definitions of the word as well as synonyms and antonynms. I then looked at quotes that either contained the word or where the meaning was present.


Below are images of my initial findings.



After researching the word and it's meaning I started to think about how I could portray it in an analogue way. I wanted to express its more literal meaning while exploring the more unusual interpretations.


Below are images of my initial ideas.



Once I had done a quick plan on paper, it became clear the routes and themes I wanted to explore. I began with one of the more obvious ideas; light shining through punched card. I wanted to try and create a 'flash of light'. Once the holes were punched in, I put the card on the lightbox to exaggerate the contrast.



My next ideas surrounded theme of destruction. I was inspired by the poem 'Belfast Confetti' by Ciaran Carson. It is about the confusion, shock and horror following a bomb explosion in Belfast. A couple of quotes stood out to me in particular; 'raining exclamation marks' and 'blocked with stops and colons'. I liked the idea of an explosion of punctuation so I decided to make my own version with letters (more in the style of Chris Ashworth). The next piece follows a similar theme, however this time I used Tip X to add a new texture. The contrast of light and dark aworks well with the subject matter.



When I was researching different quotes surrounding the word, I found one linking to flash content on the web. About 15 years ago, adobe flash was very important. It allowed people to express themselves online. I liked this concept as it was completely different. When I was thinking about how I could show this through type, I thought it would be interesting to use a code style set up. This would give the direct link to the internet. I made up some starter code for adobe flash and wrote it out in the same style on layout paper. In reflection, tracing paper may have been better. I tried to create an artificial digital environment (rather than the screen) by putting the layout paper on the lightbox. This looked really good in person, however the camera had trouble picking it up.



I started thinking back to the experimental work we did last year and began putting together some abstract typographic compositions. I wanted to highlight the effect of a 'flash' by using contrasting weights and styles of typeface. I used the typefaces Avenir, Dido and Baskerville in light, regular and bold.



My last idea was very experimental. I got inspiration for this from one night in Cockermouth. I liked the way the headlight from the cars and streetlights struck the wet road (images below) and the inconsistency between them. I wanted to explore this idea with shadows and possibly the lack of light.
