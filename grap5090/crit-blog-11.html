Session 11: Academic Presentation & Havard Referencing

In this weeks session we looked at the importance of referencing and citation with regards to essay writing. We also covered top essay writing tips.


5 Essay Writing Tips

1) Use quality sources
– Books
– Google Scholar
– One Search

2) Know what to say
– When writing an essay, make sure your personality comes through
– Consider your own stance in order to make a balanced argument
– Evaluate a range of perspectives and make your own informed judgement
– Shape your argument before you write it

3) Hone your structure
– Make sure you create a clear plan to follow (trello?)
– Introduction - 5/10% of word count. Broad overview, summarise key points
– Main Body - Chain of paragraphs, each containing a key point. Make sure that each paragraph links to create a flow to the essay
– Conclusion - 10/15% of word count. Summarise key points made and come up with a final answer to the question

4) Draft then redraft
– Draft, review, then edit
– Edit the content - you only want relevent information in the essay - stay to the point
– Have a logical structure to the essay - do all paragraphs link?
– Proofread to make sure the essay is clear and to the point
– Point, Evidence, Explain

5) Polish your tone
– Try to avoid first and second person (you and I)
– Tackle the tone in the second draft while you edit
– Aim for a similar style to which you see in readings
– Make use of the Academic Phrasebook


Referencing

You should reference a source every time you:
– Directly quote from a source
– Paraphrase from a source
– Refer to a visual source

You must be clear what is your work and what is not


Citations

Citations are inserted into the main body of an essay. (cite them right)
– Paraphrasing - Taking an idea from someone else and rewording it

Bibliography - This includes every cited source and any additional reading. Listed alphabetically by surname or organisation

– Books - Cobley, P. (2014) Narrative. 2nd Edn. London: Routledge
– Edited books - Danesi, M. (2010) 'Semiotics of media and culture' in Cobley, P. (ed.) The Routledge Companion to Semiotics. London: Routledge. pp.xx-xx
– Journal articles - Conway, S. and Elphinstone, B. (2013) ‘Towards gameworld studies’, Journal of Gaming and Virtual Worlds, 11 (3), pp.547-560
– Websites - Grinnell College (2020), Subcultures and Sociology. Available at https:// (Accessed: 4 May 2021)
- Images (website) - Fig. 1 Arbus, D. (1974) Xmas tree in a living room [Photograph] Available at: http:// (Accessed: 14 October 2018
– Images (book) - Fig. 1 Arbus, D. (1974) Xmas tree in a living room [Photograph] in San Francisco Museum of Modern Art (2003) Diane Arbus: revelations. London: Jonathon Cape p.43
