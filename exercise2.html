Exercise 2: Contrast & Negative Space

In this exercise we were asked to explore the elements of contrast and negative space in relation to magazine design. We had to create two double page spreads for a magazine. These included a splash page and a turn page. As well as only using these principles, we had to take on board what we had learned the previous week â€“ alignment and hierarchy.


Research

The first thing I did during this project was to look at examples of where contrast and negative space had been used in design. This gave me a good idea of what can be possible and also visually pleasing.



Once I had looked at contrast and negative space in general I began to look at examples of splash and turn pages. Below is an example of one I had at hand.



Thumbnails

After I had looked at different magazine layouts I sketched a few thumbnail designs for the magazine spread. This helped get my ideas onto paper and visually see what would work.



Outcomes

Once I had these ideas on paper I went over to InDesign and began putting it together on the screen. I found this process much easier as I had the thumbnails to work from. When I had completed the splash and turn pages I realised that I had only used 2 of the 3 images, so I created a second turn page to meet the requirements of the brief. Overall I am very happy with the outcomes. I tried to use all of the principles previously covered in my designs.
