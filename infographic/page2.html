Siobhan Fahey

Fahey was born in Dublin in 1958 and is a singer and musician. She was a former member of the girl group, Bananarama, but left in 1988 to pursue her own musical style and created Shakespears Sister.

Siobhan's inspiration came from many places. After spending so long working in a girl group, it took Fahey longer to identify her own style. She has been influenced by varied and diverse artists from Echo and the Bunnymen to The Smiths, to Bowie and Giorgio Moroder.

Marcella Detroit

Marcella Detroit was born in Detroit in 1952 and is an American singer, songwriter and guitarist. She co-wrote 'Lay Down Sally' with Eric Clapton in 1977 and joined Shakespears Sister in 1989.

Marcella was raised in suburban Detroit, growing up around Folk Blues and Motown. She is a well accomplished instrumentalist as well as musician, instruments ranging from the guitar to the harmonica, she has always had a very purist approach to music.
